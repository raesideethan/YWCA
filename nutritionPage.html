Allergen Information

Food labels are required to display the top 9 major allergens: Milk, Eggs, Fish, Shellfish, Tree Nuts, Peanuts, Wheat, Soy, and Sesame.

So, you may see one of these two forms:

Contains: Milk, Wheat, Soy, Peanuts

Ingredients: Enriched flour (wheat flour, niacin, iron), sugar, butter (milk), eggs.

Some brands may use icons such as the one on the left to indicate a potential allergen, however it is not required by law for companies to do.

The calories portion shows you how much energy you get per serving.

Generally, the recommended daily intake of calories for adults is 1,800 - 2,000 calories.

It is important to balance your day out and discuss with your doctor about your calorie intake.

ğŸ“Š Daily Value (%DV)

Daily value is just how much of a nutrient you are getting in one serving. It is referenced off the 2,000 calorie daily diet.

A 5% DV or less is considered low, and a 20% or more is considered high.

It is important to increase your daily value of nutrients such as:

  • Fiber
  • Vitamin D
  • Calcium
  • Iron

You should also be limiting your intake of:

  • Sodium
  • Saturated Fat
  • Added Sugars

ğŸ’ª Nutrients

Fat: needed for your energy and hormones - unsaturated fats (olive oil and nuts) should be your focus.

Saturated Fat: limit, found in butter, cheese, and fried foods.

Cholesterol: kept in moderation, found in meat, fish, eggs, etc.

Sodium: stay under 2,300 mg per day, hidden salt in sauces and snacks can become an issue.

Carbohydrates: the body's fuel, found in whole grains.

Fiber: aids digestion and helps you feel more full when you eat, found in fruits, vegetables, beans, and lentils.

Sugars: natural sugars found in fruit should be preferred.

Protein: builds muscle and repairs tissue, found in chicken, dairy, fish, etc.
